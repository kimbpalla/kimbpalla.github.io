What is Minecraft?

Minecraftis a sandbox video game developed by Mojang Studios, officially released in 2011. Known for its blocky, pixelated graphics and limitless creativity, Minecraft has become one of the best-selling video games of all time, with hundreds of millions of players across the globe.

In Minecraft, players explore a procedurally generated 3D world made of cubes, where they can gather resources, craft tools, build structures, and interact with both friendly and hostile creatures. The game offers multiple modes to suit different play styles:

Survival Mode: Players must gather resources, manage hunger, and survive against monsters.

Creative Mode: Unlimited resources and the freedom to build anything without health or hunger constraints.

Adventure Mode: Custom maps and adventures created by other players with specific rules.

Spectator Mode: Allows players to fly through blocks and observe the world without interacting.

Minecraft supports both single-player and multiplayer, fostering a massive community of modders, builders, educators, and adventurers. Its open-ended design encourages creativity, problem-solving, and collaboration, making it a popular choice in educational settings as well. Over the years, Minecraft has expanded into spin-offs (like Minecraft Dungeons), merchandise, educational editions, and even major cultural collaborations.
